# Tech Innovation Log — Week Ending 1 Feb 2026

## Major Headlines

* TikTok updates its US privacy policy as part of its ownership transition, enabling precise location tracking.
* The EU opens a formal Digital Services Act investigation into xAI over Grok generating sexualized images of women and children.
* Microsoft unveils Maia 200, its second-generation AI accelerator built on TSMC’s 3nm process, now deployed in Azure.
* Apple prepares a major AI strategy reset, planning a Gemini-powered personalized Siri and a chatbot-style Siri later in 2026.
* Apple acquires Q.ai in a deal reportedly valued at around $2B, expanding its multimodal and non-verbal AI capabilities.
* Apple reports Q1 revenue up 16% YoY to $143.8B, driven by strong iPhone sales and record growth in China.
* Meta reports Q4 revenue up 24% YoY and projects up to $135B in capital expenditures for AI and superintelligence efforts in 2026.
* Microsoft reports Q2 revenue up 17% YoY, with Azure revenue growing 39% and cloud backlog surpassing $625B.
* Amazon announces plans to cut ~16,000 corporate roles as part of organizational restructuring.
* Nvidia invests an additional $2B in CoreWeave to expand AI compute capacity beyond 5GW by 2030.
* OpenAI launches Prism, a free cloud-based LaTeX editor powered by GPT-5.2 for scientific research.
* Chinese startup Moonshot releases Kimi K2.5, an open-source multimodal agentic AI model.
* Google launches Project Genie, enabling users to create interactive AI-generated worlds.
* IonQ acquires semiconductor manufacturer SkyWater for ~$1.8B, marking its largest acquisition.
* Tesla agrees to invest $2B in xAI as part of xAI’s Series E funding round.
* SpaceX and xAI explore a potential merger ahead of a planned IPO.
* Samsung and SK Hynix report strong earnings driven by AI-related memory demand, forecasting shortages through 2027.
* Regulators clarify that tokenized stocks fall under existing securities laws.
* Waymo expands robotaxi operations but faces renewed scrutiny after a pedestrian incident.

## Minor / Emerging Headlines

* AI assistants see rapid growth in professional, scientific, and enterprise use cases.
* Data center expansion accelerates amid power, water, and labor constraints.
* Governments increase scrutiny of AI safety, content moderation, and platform accountability.
* Venture funding remains strong for AI infrastructure, robotics, autonomous vehicles, and semiconductor startups.
