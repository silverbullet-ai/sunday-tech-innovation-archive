# Tech Innovation Log — Week Ending 18 Jan 2026

## Major Headlines
- Indonesia temporarily blocks Grok, citing risks of non-consensual sexual deepfakes, becoming the first country to block the AI tool.
- Malaysia limits access to Grok, joining Indonesia over concerns related to sexual content generated by the chatbot.
- Apple signs a multiyear deal with Google to use Gemini models and Google Cloud to power future Siri features starting in 2026.
- Google confirms Gemini will power a more personalized Siri and future Apple Intelligence features.
- Alphabet reaches a $4 trillion market capitalization after its stock rises more than 6% in the past month.
- Meta confirms layoffs in Reality Labs and says savings will be reinvested into wearables.
- Meta closes three VR gaming studios and halts new development for its VR fitness app Supernatural.
- Reports say Meta and EssilorLuxottica are discussing significantly expanding Ray-Ban Meta smart glasses production capacity by 2026.
- Google launches Personal Intelligence, a Gemini feature that connects with Gmail, Photos, Search, and YouTube to deliver personalized responses for paid users.
- A source alleges a former Thinking Machines Lab executive shared confidential company information before returning to OpenAI.
- OpenAI plans to test ads below ChatGPT replies for free and Go-tier users in the US, aiming to generate billions in ad revenue in 2026.
- OpenAI expands its ChatGPT Go subscription tier globally, pricing it at $8 per month in the US.

## Minor / Emerging Headlines
- Elon Musk criticizes the Apple–Google Gemini partnership, citing concerns about concentration of power.
- Google emphasizes privacy controls and user consent in Gemini’s Personal Intelligence integrations.
- OpenAI says ads in ChatGPT will be clearly labeled, will not influence responses, and will not expose conversations to advertisers.
