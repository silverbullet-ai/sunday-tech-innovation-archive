# Tech Innovation Log — Week Ending 25th Jan 2026

## Major Headlines

* Elon Musk files a lawsuit seeking $79B–$134B in damages from OpenAI and Microsoft, alleging OpenAI abandoned its nonprofit mission.
* Sequoia plans a major investment in Anthropic as part of a funding round targeting more than $25B.
* Micron breaks ground on a New York memory manufacturing complex, set to become the largest semiconductor facility in the US.
* Micron signs a $1.8B agreement to acquire a fabrication site in Taiwan to expand DRAM production.
* The EU plans cybersecurity rules expected to phase out high-risk vendors such as Huawei from critical infrastructure.
* OpenAI rolls out age prediction globally on ChatGPT to apply automatic protections for users under 18.
* OpenAI plans to test ads below ChatGPT replies for free and Go-tier users, targeting billions in revenue by 2026.
* Google launches Personal Intelligence, a Gemini feature that connects with Gmail, Photos, Search, and YouTube to deliver personalized AI responses.
* Meta confirms layoffs in Reality Labs and shifts investment toward wearables.
* Meta plans to significantly expand Ray-Ban Meta smart glasses production capacity by 2026.
* Apple signs a multiyear deal with Google to use Gemini models and Google Cloud to power future Siri and Apple Intelligence features.
* Apple is reportedly developing an AI wearable pin with cameras and microphones, potentially launching as early as 2027.
* Apple plans to revamp Siri into a full AI chatbot in iOS 27 and macOS 27, powered by Google’s Gemini.
* Alphabet reaches a $4T market capitalization amid rising investor confidence.
* Nvidia H200 chip shipments are reportedly delayed after Chinese customs blocked AI processor imports.
* Data centers are expected to consume over 70% of global high-end memory chip output in 2026.
* South Korea enacts the AI Basic Act, introducing one of the world’s first comprehensive AI regulatory frameworks.
* TikTok says ByteDance sold a majority stake in its US operations to a group of non-Chinese investors.
* Waymo launches its robotaxi service in Miami, expanding to its sixth market.
* Tesla launches robotaxi rides in Austin with no human safety driver.
* The NYSE plans to enable real-time trading and on-chain settlement of tokenized securities in 2026.
* Ukraine plans to share wartime drone and combat data with allies to help train AI military models.
* X open sources its core recommendation system, stating it is powered by a Grok-based transformer model.
